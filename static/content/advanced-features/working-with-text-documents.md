# Working with Text Documents

LexLuma allows users to **upload and interact with text-based documents** for efficient legal research. Text documents include plain `.txt` files, Word documents (`.docx`), and other editable formats.

---

## 📝 Key Capabilities

- **Full-Text Search:** Instantly locate clauses, sections, or keywords across your legal documents.  
- **Summarization:** Generate concise overviews of statutes, rulings, or contracts.  
- **Question-Answering:** Ask specific legal questions and receive references to the document sections.  
- **Context-Aware Analysis:** LexLuma remembers prior chat context, enabling multi-turn document queries.

---

## 🔧 How to Work with Text Documents

1. Upload text documents via the **Documents Panel**.  
2. Tag each document with **category, case type, or jurisdiction**.  
3. Start a new chat and select the document as your context.  
4. Ask targeted questions, e.g., “Summarize key obligations in this contract.”  
5. Review AI responses and use **message actions** to edit or regenerate answers as needed.

---

## 💡 Best Practices

- Use **consistent tagging and naming conventions** to organize multiple documents.  
- Break long text files into sections for better clarity and context retention.  
- Combine **text document chat** with PDFs or vision models for hybrid research workflows.  
- Regularly **update document versions** to ensure your AI references the most recent legal materials.
