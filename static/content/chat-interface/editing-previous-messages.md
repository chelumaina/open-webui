# Editing Previous Messages

LexLuma allows you to **edit your previously sent messages**, enabling precise control over prompts and reducing repetitive typing. This is especially helpful when conducting detailed legal research, where even minor changes in wording can significantly influence the accuracy of responses.

---

## ✏️ Why Edit Messages?

Editing is useful when:
- You forgot to include a fact or citation  
- The prompt contains a typo or ambiguity  
- You want to refine the legal issue for analysis  
- You wish to adjust instructions without starting a new thread  

Instead of rewriting your entire question, editing lets you improve the prompt and immediately request a revised answer.

---

## 🛠️ How to Edit a Message

1. Hover over your message.  
2. Click the **Edit** option from the Message Actions Menu.  
3. Update the text.  
4. Submit the edited prompt.  

LexLuma will then:
- Update your message in place  
- Generate a new response based on the revised wording  

The old answer remains visible for comparison.

---

## 🧠 Best Practices for Editing

### **1. Clarify the Legal Issue**
Provide explicit instructions:

Edit: Focus on constitutional grounds only.


### **2. Add Missing Facts**
Legal reasoning improves dramatically with complete information.

### **3. Adjust Tone or Output Format**
Great for drafting:
- Case briefs  
- Memos  
- Opinions  
- Submissions  

### **4. Use Editing for Iterative Research**
Gradually refine your prompt rather than starting from zero.
