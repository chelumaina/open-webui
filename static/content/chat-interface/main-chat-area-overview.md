# Main Chat Area Overview

The **Main Chat Area** is the core workspace of LexLuma, where all legal research interactions, document analyses, and AI-assisted queries occur. It is designed to be clean, distraction-free, and optimized for high-accuracy legal workflows. Whether you're drafting arguments, interpreting statutes, or exploring case law, this area provides all the tools needed for efficient and reliable AI-driven research.

---

## 🖥️ Key Interface Zones

The Main Chat Area is divided into three functional regions:

### **1. Conversation Stream**
This is the vertical thread where your discussion with LexLuma unfolds. Messages appear sequentially, preserving the context of your legal inquiry.  
Each message bubble clearly differentiates:
- **User messages** (right-aligned or highlighted)
- **AI responses** (left-aligned with LexLuma branding)

### **2. Input Composer**
Located at the bottom of the screen, this is where you write queries, request analysis, or upload legal documents.  
It supports:
- Multi-line drafting  
- Markdown formatting  
- File attachment icons  
- Shortcut keys for quick interactions  

For more details, view:  
🔗 *See:* [/chat-interface/composing-messages](#)

### **3. Tools & Quick Actions Toolbar**
Depending on your workflow, you may see optional actions such as:
- **Model switching**
- **Regenerate Response**
- **Message Actions Menu**
- **Export tools** (PDF, DOCX)
- **RAG source citations panel** (if enabled)

---

## 📘 What the Chat Area Is Designed For

LexLuma is trained on **legislation, gazettes, court decisions, and policy materials**, making it ideal for:
- Drafting pleadings  
- Analyzing statutes and precedents  
- Extracting issues from case files  
- Summarizing bulky legal documents  
- Comparing sections across jurisdictions  

---

## ⭐ Tips for Effective Use
- Provide **context**, such as jurisdiction or legal issue.  
- Upload relevant documents for targeted analysis.  
- Use the **message actions** to refine or extend AI answers.  
- Keep conversations organized—name chats according to matters.  
- Use **RAG citations** to verify where insights come from.
