# Reading Conversations

Reading conversations effectively in LexLuma is essential for building a clear mental model of ongoing legal matters, understanding referenced documents, and reliably tracing the AI’s reasoning. LexLuma’s conversation stream is structured to feel intuitive and to ensure accuracy, transparency, and efficiency for lawyers, clerks, and legal researchers.

---

## 🧭 Understanding the Conversation Layout

Each chat is displayed as a **scrollable timeline** of messages, giving you a chronological overview of interactions. Messages are styled distinctly to help you quickly identify who said what:

- **Your Messages** — Highlighted and right-aligned.  
- **LexLuma Responses** — Left-aligned and marked with LexLuma’s visual identity.  

Important metadata such as timestamps and message actions are always available but kept visually unobtrusive.

---

## 🧩 RAG Citations and Legal Sources

When LexLuma references legislation, gazettes, or court decisions pulled from the RAG system, source links appear directly beneath the response. This ensures:
- Transparency  
- Verifiability  
- Professional reliability  

You can click citations to preview or open full documents.

---

## 📚 Tracking Conversation Context

LexLuma is context-aware. As long as you remain in the same conversation, it:
- Understands your ongoing matter  
- Remembers uploaded evidence or documents  
- Maintains continuity across several questions  

For example, after uploading a contract or ruling, you may ask:  
> “Identify ambiguous clauses,”  
and later follow with:  
> “Draft a clearer version of clause 7.”

LexLuma uses the existing context unless you clear or switch chats.

---

## 🔍 Zooming Into Details

Hover or tap any message to reveal:
- **Expand/Collapse** tools  
- **Copy text**  
- **View sources**  
- **Regenerate**  
- **Delete or edit your message**

This improves traceability, especially for complex multi-layered legal analyses.
