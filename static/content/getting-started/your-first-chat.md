# Your First Chat

Starting your first research conversation with LexLuma is simple, intuitive, and designed to replicate the natural workflow of legal reasoning. This section guides you through creating, managing, and optimizing your first AI interaction.

---

## 🚀 Step 1: Creating a New Chat

From the sidebar, click **New Chat**.  
A fresh workspace opens where you can ask your first question—for example:

- “Explain the elements of breach of contract.”
- “Summarize this judgment,” followed by uploading the case.
- “Compare Section X of Act A with Section Y of Act B.”

LexLuma uses its RAG-backed knowledge base to return accurate, context-grounded insights.

---

## 📄 Step 2: Uploading Documents

You can upload materials directly into your chat:

- Court decisions  
- Gazette notices  
- Legislative excerpts  
- Legal memos or pleadings  

LexLuma can then extract key issues, summarize content, identify legal principles, or analyze arguments.

---

## 🔎 Step 3: Asking Follow-up Questions

Each chat maintains **context**, so you can continue:

- “Explain that holding in simpler language.”  
- “How does this compare with precedent X?”  
- “List the key legal issues raised.”  

The conversation flows naturally, similar to speaking with an experienced researcher.

---

## 💼 Step 4: Managing Your Research Threads

All chats are automatically saved. You can **rename** each conversation to organize by matter:

- *“Land Dispute – Evidence Issues”*  
- *“Judicial Review – Procedural Steps”*  

This helps build a structured archive of research notes.
