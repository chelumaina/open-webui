# AI-Assisted Legal Writing Best Practices

AI can improve legal writing by generating drafts, enhancing clarity, reducing passive voice, and ensuring consistency. However, effective use requires structured practices. Begin by defining the goal of the document—motion, memo, email, or contract—and provide the AI with context, jurisdiction, and tone expectations.

---
Always review AI-generated drafts for accuracy, especially when dealing with facts, citations, or procedural details. Edit for clarity, logical structure, and client-specific nuances. AI excels at rewriting sections to improve readability, but attorneys must ensure legal precision.

---
Use AI for:

    - Draft outlines
    - Rewriting for clarity
    - Formatting citations
    - Generating alternative clause language
    - Creating summaries of lengthy documents


---
Avoid relying solely on AI for legal reasoning, issue spotting, or argument framing. Treat AI as an assistant that enhances efficiency but requires thorough attorney supervision.
