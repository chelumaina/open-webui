# Avoiding Common Pitfalls in Legal AI Use

Despite its advantages, legal AI can introduce risks if not used carefully. The most common pitfall is over-reliance on AI outputs without verifying citations or legal reasoning. Lawyers must independently evaluate results to avoid incorporating inaccuracies or hallucinations into filings.


---
Another frequent mistake is uploading confidential documents without ensuring the AI platform meets required security standards. Always confirm encryption, data residency, and non-retention policies before sharing sensitive materials. 


---
Poorly structured prompts can also lead to incomplete or misleading answers. Use clear instructions specifying jurisdiction, date ranges, and desired format. Similarly, mixing unrelated documents in a single workspace can confuse RAG systems.


---
Legal teams may also overlook the importance of keeping training materials and workflows current. Outdated templates or old case files can degrade the accuracy of AI-assisted work. Establish routine review cycles to maintain system performance.


---
By anticipating these pitfalls and implementing safeguards, legal professionals can maximize AI benefits while minimizing risk.
