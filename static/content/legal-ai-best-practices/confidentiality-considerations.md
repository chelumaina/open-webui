# Confidentiality Considerations with Legal AI

Protecting client confidentiality is the cornerstone of the attorney-client relationship and a fundamental ethical obligation. When using AI tools like LexLuma AI, special care must be taken to safeguard sensitive information.

## Key Principles

*   **Assume Data is Processed:** Operate under the assumption that any text, document, or image you input into a public AI model may be used for training purposes and could be potentially accessible to third parties.
*   **The Principle of Least Exposure:** Only provide the minimum amount of information necessary for the AI to complete the task effectively.
*   **You are the Guardian:** Ultimately, you are responsible for any disclosure of confidential information, regardless of the tool used.

## Practical Do's and Don'ts

### ✅ DO:

*   **De-identify Data:** Before inputting client information, remove or redact all personally identifiable information (PII), case names, specific financial figures, and any other unique identifiers. Use placeholders like "[Client Name]" or "[Specific Dollar Amount]".
*   **Use Abstract Hypotheticals:** Frame your questions and research around abstract legal issues rather than the specifics of a live case.
*   **Leverage Secure, Enterprise Tools:** Prefer AI tools like LexLuma AI that offer enterprise-grade data handling agreements, ensuring that your data is not used for model training and is protected by robust security measures.
*   **Verify Platform Policies:** Thoroughly read and understand the privacy policy and terms of service of any AI platform you use.

### ❌ DO NOT:

*   **Input Full, Unredacted Documents:** Never copy and paste entire client emails, contracts, pleadings, or discovery documents that contain confidential information.
*   **Reveal Client Identities:** Avoid prompts that include a client's name, business, or any details that could lead to their identification.
*   **Use Public, Unsecured AI Chatbots for Sensitive Work:** Free or public-facing AI chatbots typically present the highest risk for data exposure.
