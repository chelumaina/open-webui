# Ethical Use of AI in Law  
AI has become a powerful tool for legal professionals, but its usage must always align with established ethical standards. This includes obligations under professional codes of conduct, confidentiality rules, client transparency expectations, and data protection regulations.


---

## ⚖️ Core Ethical Principles

### **1. Competence**
Lawyers must understand the capabilities and limitations of AI tools to use them responsibly. This includes knowing when AI may enhance research and when human expertise must override automated outputs.

### **2. Confidentiality**
Client information must be safeguarded at all times. Any AI interaction involving sensitive material must follow strict confidentiality protocols (see *Confidentiality Considerations*).

### **3. Transparency**
Clients may need to be informed when AI tools significantly contribute to research, drafting, or analysis. Transparency builds trust and avoids ethical ambiguity.

### **4. Accuracy and Verification**
AI outputs must never be accepted blindly. Every statute, case citation, summary, or interpretation generated by AI must be verified manually.


---

## 🧰 Practical Ethical Guidelines

- Use AI as an assistant, not a decision-maker.  
- Keep detailed notes of how AI contributed to your work.  
- Avoid relying on AI for ambiguous or jurisdiction-specific interpretations.  
- Do not delegate attorney-client decision-making to automated tools.  
- Review and correct AI drafts before filing or presenting material.


---

## 🧭 When to Use Human Judgment

AI is helpful for:
- summarization  
- early-stage research  
- drafting outlines  
- identifying relevant precedents  

But **human legal expertise** is required for:
- interpreting statutory intention  
- constructing arguments  
- evaluating risks  
- advising clients
