# Measuring the Impact of AI on Legal Practice

Evaluating AI’s impact requires tracking key performance indicators (KPIs) across the firm. Begin by comparing time spent on research, drafting, and review tasks before and after AI implementation. Many firms see reductions of 30–60% in drafting time and significant improvements in research turnaround.

---
Quality indicators include accuracy improvements, reduced drafting errors, and enhanced consistency across documents. Measure client satisfaction by tracking response times, communication quality, and overall engagement.

---
Operational metrics such as reduced backlog, increased case capacity, and improved attorney utilization rates also reveal AI’s value. For transactional practices, track how quickly contracts are reviewed or due diligence tasks are completed. For litigation, monitor improvements in brief preparation and evidence analysis.

---
Regularly gather attorney feedback to identify bottlenecks and highlight features that offer the greatest benefit. Measuring impact ensures ongoing optimization and helps justify investment in AI tools across the practice.
