# Optimizing Voice Features for Legal Use

Voice features can streamline legal workflows by enabling hands-free drafting, dictation, note-taking, and matter updates. To maximize productivity, legal professionals should establish structured voice usage habits and tailor the environment for clear, accurate transcription. Begin by using high-quality microphones and minimizing background noise, especially during dictation of sensitive or complex material. Speak clearly and at a moderate pace; legal terminology is often specialized and benefits from precise enunciation.

---
Create standardized voice commands for recurring activities, such as “summarize deposition notes,” “draft email to client,” or “extract deadlines from this document.” Consistency helps the AI better predict intent and return reliable results. When recording meetings, interviews, or client calls, ensure you have informed consent to comply with privacy and regulatory requirements. Enable automatic timestamps, speaker identification, and action-item extraction to enhance matter management.

---
After generating transcriptions, always perform a review to correct misheard legal terms, names, or citations. Store approved voice transcriptions in your document management system and tag them with matter numbers or categories for easy retrieval. For high-risk legal work such as litigation filings or transactional drafting, treat voice-generated content only as a first draft, refining it manually before use. By integrating voice features thoughtfully, legal teams can significantly increase efficiency while maintaining compliance and accuracy.
