# Risk Management Strategies  
Integrating AI into legal workflows requires a structured approach to identifying, evaluating, and mitigating risks. Although LexLuma provides a secure, reliable environment for legal tasks, professionals must actively manage AI-related risk in their own practice.


---

## 🛡 Key Risks When Using AI in Law  
### **1. Accuracy Risks**  
AI may generate incomplete or outdated legal interpretations without proper oversight.

### **2. Confidentiality Risks**  
Incorrectly handling sensitive data could compromise privileged information.

### **3. Workflow Risks**  
Overreliance on AI may weaken internal review processes or lead to procedural shortcuts.

### **4. Bias and Misinterpretation**  
AI may reflect biases in source materials or interpret ambiguous language improperly.

### **5. Compliance Risks**  
Failure to follow jurisdictional regulations or disclosure requirements.


---

## 🧰 Risk Mitigation Strategies

### **1. Mandatory Human Review**  
Require at least one experienced attorney to verify all AI-assisted outputs.

### **2. Controlled Access to Sensitive Data**  
Use access-controlled LexLuma workspaces to limit who can read or modify data.

### **3. Prompt Design Guidelines**  
Establish internal standards for prompting, such as:  
- avoiding the inclusion of sensitive case details  
- requesting citations for all legal claims  
- using redacted documents when possible  

### **4. Versioning & Documentation**  
Keep organized records of AI-generated drafts and human-reviewed versions.

### **5. Sanity Checks & Multi-Query Validation**  
Ask the AI:  
- to list assumptions  
- to highlight uncertainties  
- to check jurisdiction  
- to verify citations  

### **6. Clear Escalation Paths**  
Determine when an issue must be escalated from AI-assisted work to expert analysis.


---

## 📌 Risk Monitoring Tools  
- Compliance checklists  
- Prompt logs  
- Model choice history  
- Workspace audit logs  
- Automated citation extraction
