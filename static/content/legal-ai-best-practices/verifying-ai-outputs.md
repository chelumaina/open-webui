# Verifying AI Outputs  
LexLuma provides rapid analysis and retrieval of legal information, but accuracy must always be verified by human professionals. AI can surface insights, summarize documents, and locate statutes, but it should never replace legal verification processes.


---

## 🔍 Why Verification Matters

AI models generate content based on patterns and retrieved sources. While LexLuma enhances accuracy through RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation), mistakes can still occur, including:
- incorrect citations  
- outdated laws  
- missing jurisdiction details  
- incomplete summaries  
- subtle misinterpretations  


---

## 🧰 Recommended Verification Workflow

### **1. Request Sources**
Use prompts like:  
> “List the exact sections/statutes you used to generate this answer.”  
> “Provide the citations with links.”

### **2. Compare Against Official Materials**
Always compare AI-generated references with:
- official court websites  
- authenticated legislative databases  
- gazette publications  
- printed statute books  

### **3. Use Multi-Step Verification**
Break the process into:
1. **AI summary**  
2. **AI citation extraction**  
3. **Manual source validation**  
4. **Human interpretation**

### **4. Cross-Check with Multiple Queries**
Ask follow-up questions such as:
- “Are there exceptions to this ruling?”  
- “What is the latest amendment?”  
- “Does this apply in X jurisdiction?”


---

## ⚠️ Red Flags to Watch For

- overly confident explanations without citations  
- long summaries missing key legal reasoning  
- references to non-existent case numbers  
- mismatched jurisdictions  


---

## 📌 Verification Checklist

| Step | Status |
|------|--------|
| AI provided citations | ✔ |
| Citations validated manually | ✔ |
| Jurisdiction confirmed | ✔ |
| Human-reviewed interpretation | ✔ |
