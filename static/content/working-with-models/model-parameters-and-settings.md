# Model Parameters and Settings

LexLuma allows users to customize **model parameters** to control response behavior, output style, and depth of analysis. Proper configuration ensures AI responses are optimized for specific legal tasks.

---

## 🔧 Key Model Parameters

### **1. Temperature**
Controls randomness and creativity. Lower values produce precise answers, higher values encourage creative interpretations.

### **2. Maximum Response Length**
Determines how detailed a response should be. Useful for drafting memos or summarizing lengthy rulings.

### **3. Context Window**
Specifies how much prior conversation the AI can consider. Longer windows help with multi-step legal reasoning.

### **4. Top-p / Nucleus Sampling**
Limits token selection probability, refining response focus. Useful for legal precision.

---

## 🛠️ Adjusting Settings

- Open the **Model Settings Panel** in the chat.  
- Modify each parameter according to your research needs.  
- Save settings to apply for current and future chats.  

---

## 💡 Best Practices

- Use **low temperature** for statutes, court rulings, or technical legal drafts.  
- Increase **response length** for comprehensive summaries or case comparisons.  
- Adjust **context window** if analyzing long multi-part cases.  
- Experiment with **Top-p** settings for nuanced reasoning in complex legal scenarios.
