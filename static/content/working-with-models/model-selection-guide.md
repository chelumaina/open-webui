# Model Selection Guide

LexLuma provides a variety of AI models tailored to different legal research tasks. Choosing the right model ensures accurate interpretations, faster responses, and highly relevant results for lawyers, clerks, and legislators.

---

## 🔍 Factors to Consider When Selecting a Model

### **1. Task Type**
- **Text Analysis:** Choose text-only models for reading, summarizing, or drafting legal documents.  
- **Document Understanding:** Vision-enabled models handle scanned statutes, gazettes, or exhibits.  
- **Specialized Tasks:** Use coding or creative models for complex reasoning, contract automation, or scenario drafting.

### **2. Response Depth**
- Basic models are faster and provide concise responses.  
- Advanced models deliver detailed legal analysis and reasoning.

### **3. Context Awareness**
- Larger models support longer context windows, useful for multi-step case analyses.  
- Smaller models are ideal for quick queries or short document reviews.

---

## 🧰 Model Selection Tips

- **Start with default suggestions:** LexLuma automatically recommends suitable models based on your query type.  
- **Review model descriptions:** Click the model panel for capabilities and limitations.  
- **Switch if needed:** Models can be changed mid-conversation without losing context.  
- **Combine models:** Use specialized models for specific tasks, such as summarizing cases or drafting legal arguments.

---

## 📘 Best Practices

- Define your legal task clearly before selecting a model.  
- Consider the size and complexity of your document.  
- For critical legal research, cross-check results with multiple models.  
- Take advantage of **RAG citations** to validate sources.
